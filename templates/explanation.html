Explanation of Game Mechanics

Sokoban is a classic puzzle game orignally created by Hiroyuki Imabayashi (1982) where the player pushes boxes around a space, trying to get them to designated locations.
The player can only push one box at a time and cannot pull boxes. The must push these boxes into the designed target positions.
The goal is to move all boxes to their target positions in the least number of moves and time.

The following symbolisation is used in the game:

  • Player: The character you control, represented by a green triangle.
  • Boxes: The squares you need to push, represented by a blue square.
  • Goals: The designated positions for the boxes, represented by a red square.
As well as the following controls:
  • Arrow Keys: Move the player in the corresponding direction.
  • Z: Undo a move
  • X: Redo a move
**Note:** If the game is not responding to input, try clicking on the game area to focus it.
